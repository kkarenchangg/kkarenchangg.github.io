Overview

This case study covers my initial steps of creating Dot, an application that combines the convenience of a mobile calendar with the comprehension and customization of a written bullet journal. It brings the convenience of having your daily schedule at your fingertips at all times, alongside tools to help you stay focused and productive. This tool was designed for the lifestyle of students that seek organization for their classes to busy adults balancing work and life.

Problem / Research

There are many different planners offered that organize various parts of our life — our schedule, meals, sleep, mood, etc. — but it seems as though there is not one planner that satisfies all of our needs. Even if there was one, it would not be available everywhere you go. Online calendars provide convenience and different layouts for your day, week, month or year views. Bullet journals, on the other hand, allow the user to create any layout they would like, and stretch their creativity to track and plan any and all parts of their life.

Reviews about Google Calendar have said that the simple interface sometimes comes at the cost of not being able to fully personalize the app - such as adding a tasks bar to jot down something needed for an event coming up. While calendar apps are great for scheduling purposes, they start to become less practical when needing to schedule meetings, incorporate attachments, plan for events, and include personal items in once place. Users have also mentioned that they would like to see greater capabilities in personalizing their calendars and layouts to fit their needs and styles. Physical bullet journals could be a solution to this issue as the user can incorporate everything they need for the day. However, this planning method does carry some of their own downfalls, including how much time it takes to recreate a layout, or "spread" each week, how inconvenient it can be to fix or edit events that can easily change throughout the week, or how they are not as portable or accessible in many situations. What would be ideal is a virtual bullet journal that can not only incorporate any or all tools to plan, track, and schedule each day to month, but also be accessible on any device at anytime without needing to manually draw out grids and tables in a book for each week.‍

Goals

The goals of this application are the following:
- To create a space that combines different ways to organize life through scheduling, lists, and trackers
- To offer personalization of physical bullet journals with the convenience of calendar applications
- To make planning and organizing fun and creative for each user
- To be accessible on web, mobile, and desktop applications.

Targeted Audience

This application is geared towards people seeking to organize various aspects in their life at once:
- A student that needs a planner for their upcoming assignments and tests
- A business owner overseeing their company's needs
- And many more individuals
The Dot Application is to be a tool available to the user throughout the day, in order to be a digital and personal organizer without needing to hop between multiple apps for different needs.

iteration & planning

Beginning with a diagram flow, I started with the potential paths that a user may take throughout a visit. When first tapping into the application, the user can choose to open any of their customized planner views, or edit/add events as they go.

logo & Visual identity

The name "Dot" was chosen to represent the dots found on bullet journals - they are the essential building block of this type of planning, as well as the inspiration for the block layout of this application. It is also a nod to the phrase "on the dot," as this application helps the user be exactly that.

Solution

The Dot Application is the assistant that molds to any person's lifestyle and needs. Available on Mobile/Desktop/Web, Dot allows the user to keep track of anything they may want at the palm of their hands.

Dot provides the fully comprehensive run-down of the user's day with the capability to add a section, see other users' days, or edit any information that the user may need to organize their schedules and goals.

Thoughts / Next steps

This case study was run solely myself, and I would love to do more research with a pool of users to see how this application can increase productivity by providing one space for the user's whole day. This idea has been something that I personally really wanted to have, as I use a hand-written bullet journal and plan out my schedule manually. It has been a long journey of trying to find the perfect planner that truly captured everything I wished to remember, but at the end of the day it was best to just create a planner myself.

Moving forward, and possibly into development, I want to refresh the way the tools can be used and try out more iterations of the menu layouts. I think that the main page layout is a success because there is a view of everything available, but I do wish to show more options of customization of the design and specifications a user could place on each section of their Dot planner.

Overview

Working with our team of designers, web project manager, and web developer at Raindrop Marketing, I created a direction for the Souplantation/Sweet Tomatoes Restaurant homepage. This was one direction presented as a solution to the needs and preferences the brand had, because the client wanted to redesign their site to match their new marketing campaign that we also created for them.

brand notes / Goals

Through their website remodel, Souplantation/Sweet Tomatoes wanted to achieve a handful of goals:
- Stand out as a relevant industry leader with their new branding
- Highlight their workers' stories
- Create a modern site that is interactive & fun, yet simple and easy to navigate
- Intentionally showcase seasonal items
‍
The site directions all follow the new brand guideline of fonts and colors while still maintaining the same familiar logos the client already had.

Targeted Audiences

Souplantation/Sweet Tomatoes was looking to branch out towards the younger family dynamic with their new branding. Their past advertising and branding catered very well to older audiences, but they wanted to also incorporate a youthful approach to invite younger parents to bring their families to the restaurants.

Wireframes / iteration

The wireframes had to include various aspects that were interactive yet informative. For example, hover effects were added to incorporate interest and movement to the page without overwhelming the user. In addition, parallelograms were used rather than regular blocks for each section to incorporate something that could be visually appealing to younger ages.

There were two directions that the menu panels could be: exposed and fixed or hidden, and the exposed menu was ultimately chosen to allow for easiest and quickest navigation. A section was also dedicated to the current month's seasonal menus in order to introduce the new items.

Lastly, another section was incorporated to introduce the new ad campaign. These ads contained different characters made from various flavor combinations, and incorporating an interactive aspect to the site using these characters would be able to help the new campaign branding be more recognizable to the user.

Thoughts / Next steps

This direction was incredibly enjoyable to create. I was given a blank state to have fun with as this client wanted something catered toward younger families, so I wanted to add ways for the site to be interactive and informative simultaneously. I was able to have a few rounds of review and feedback for this design before the direction was chosen.
‍
This project was my first full web page redesign using a newly established brand guideline. I was able to learn how to proportion various sections, text, and buttons to create hierarchy throughout the page, and train myself to think about what the user would see in a specific viewport rather than the screen as a whole. Moving forward, I would like to work on the transitions between sections — to avoid overlap of photos — and learn more ways of incorporating interaction without overwhelming the user. If given more time I would want to continue with the mobile design of this webpage and see how the transitions between sections would condense with the smaller screen sizes.

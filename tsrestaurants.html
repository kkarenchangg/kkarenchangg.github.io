T S Restaurants

Marketing Collateral and Website Design
Agency: Raindrop Marketing Agency
Adobe Photoshop, XD, Illustrator

Overview

The T S Restaurant Family is a group of 13 restaurants located throughout Hawaii and California. My role at Raindrop Marketing consisted of creating various marketing collateral and digital assets for these restaurants including menu design, event materials, print and digital advertisements, and email marketing under tight deadlines. T S Restaurants was a high-volume client with tight deadlines. As their designer, I provided efficient and accurate work to ensure that each restaurant got their items on time. I teamed up with the two brand managers for this specific client, two designers, copywriters, photographers/content team for the media assets, and our web designer to update the business' website.

Goals

The ultimate goal of the custom print advertisements, emails, and websites was to promote increased CTR and restaurant visitation. Each email announced a new food item or event offered by the restaurant and encouraged their visitors to stop by. My personal goal was to use custom emails as practice to stretch my creativity with a blank canvas and copy to begin with.

I collaborated with our team's web designer to update his original designs for the Duke and Sunnyside homepages. These updates included creating a handful of iterations for client to choose from, protoyping on Adobe XD, and finally preparing the website package to be pushed live by our web team.

Thoughts / What I learned

Working on these assets definitely taught me the importance of building relationships with our clients. T S Restaurants considers Raindrop as their Ohana, and the choices made for some projects were very intentional to keep Hawaiian tradition, including the choices on various branding opportunities, event designs, and advertisements. These emails were initially one of the most intimidating tasks given to me, as I was told to just use a few lines of text to create an eye-catching email. However, thanks to the high volume of these projects and great mentors, custom emails became one of my favorite tasks to receive. I am excited to see how far I have come as a designer to have "conquered" the digital realm of custom eblasts. Working on custom emails and webpage updates has also helped me learn the transition between creating print and digital assets — and how to think with the user primarily in mind — especially as I was cultivating my career towards UX/UI Design as well.
